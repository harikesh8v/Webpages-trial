About Me

Where are you from? For me, an India-born, Oman-raised individual - such a question does not always have a straightforward answer.
My name is Harikeshav Pramodu. I am a 20-year-old, third culture kid brought up in Oman, a country on the southeastern coast of the Arabian Peninsula in Western Asia and the oldest independent state in the Arab world.
It has never been easy for me to answer this question, and it almost makes me feel uneasy to explain what it feels like to be born in one country and be brought up somewhere else amidst a completely different culture and people.

Although I am an Indian, a significant part of who I am is somehow deeply rooted in the beautiful Sultanate of Oman. Being a third culture kid has allowed me to enjoy the best of both worlds, i.e. the Omani culture and the Indian culture. I will never be able to choose between them both. I believe that both cultures have had a significant influence in shaping me into who I am today, making me highly adaptable, culturally savvy and open-minded.

I am an enterprising Business Administration student with a passion for sports, data analytics and policymaking. I am very ambitious and goal-oriented; however, I make sure not to miss out on my dosage of fun. One of my most substantial assets is my leadership skill, which can be credited to the plethora of experience I have had in holding prominent positions of responsibility, representing and leading a large group of Individuals, among others. I believe in the 'Law of Attraction' and the 'deep-rooted connection of each being to the universe'; these ideologies help me see the best in everything and stay positive.

"One day you'll leave this world behind
So live a life you will remember"
- The Nights, Avicci

The above snippet from the song, 'The Nights' by Avicci has been my go-to- mantra in my life and something that I have lived by. I make sure to cherish and savour every moment in my life and try to make the best out of it. This line keeps me motivated and encourages me to strive forward whenever I feel low. I somehow think that this snippet is an invaluable part of my life and, in turn, a summation of who I am and how I want to live my life.
